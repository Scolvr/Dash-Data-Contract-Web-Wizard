Token Owner & Name

Identify yourself as the contract owner and give your token its identity.

Guide

Start by entering your owner identity ID, then provide your token name. Without a valid identity ID, you cannot proceed to registration.

What to Enter

  1. Owner Identity ID

    REQUIRED FIRST: Your Base58-encoded Dash Platform identity ID (43-44 characters). This permanently identifies you as the contract owner.

    Example: "4hKFP3mFB9vku8VJKcZvwVN123..."

    ‚ö†Ô∏è Without a valid identity ID, you cannot access the Registration step.

  2. Token Name

    Choose a unique and descriptive name for your token (2‚Äì64 characters). This is the primary identifier that users will see across all platforms and interfaces.

    Example: "Mega Awesome Token" or "MyProject Coin"

  3. Plural Form

    Specify how multiple units of your token will be displayed in user interfaces.

    Example: If token name is "Token", plural could be "Tokens" or "Coins"

  4. Capitalization

Best Practices

  • Keep your name short, clear, and easy to remember.
  • Avoid using symbols or special characters unless necessary.
  • The chosen name will be visible to all users and cannot be easily changed after deployment.
  • Consider how the name sounds when spoken and how it appears in different contexts.

Name Updates

Control whether your token's name or conventions can be modified after launch.

Guide

Choose who can change naming rules:

  • Contract Owner (you) - Only you can make changes
  • Specific Identity (ID-based) - Delegate to another trusted identity
  • Group (shared control) - Multi-signature governance
  • No One (fully locked) - Names cannot be changed by anyone, ever

If enabled, decide who can change this rule in the future. Click Continue to define permissions.

What This Controls

This permission determines whether your token's display conventions can be updated after deployment, including:

  • Token name (singular and plural forms)
  • Localization strings for different languages
  • Capitalization preferences
  • Decimals display (in some configurations)

Best Practices

  • Early-stage projects: Enable changes with Owner or Group control for flexibility during development.
  • Mature projects: Lock changes (set to "No One") after establishing brand identity and listings.
  • DAO tokens: Use Group control for community governance over naming decisions.
  • Enterprise tokens: Assign to Specific Identity for compliance or regulatory control.

Important Notes

Warning: Setting this to "No One" is permanent and cannot be reversed. Your token's name will be locked forever.

Rebranding: If you anticipate potential rebranding, keep this permission enabled with appropriate controls.

Supply & Basics

Set your token's base parameters ‚Äî supply, decimals, and activity tracking.

Detailed Guide

Decimals

Defines how divisible your token is.

Example: 2 decimals ‚Üí smallest unit = 0.01. 18 decimals (common for ERC-20 style tokens) ‚Üí very fine granularity.

What it means: More decimals = smoother user experience for micro-transactions but slightly higher computational precision cost.

Base Supply

The amount of tokens created at launch. You can later mint more if minting is enabled.

Max Supply

Hard cap for total possible tokens. If enabled, no minting can exceed this cap.

Warning: Irreversible once deployed if you lock rules.

Changing Max Supply

Decide whether the maximum supply limit can be modified after deployment. This gives flexibility to adjust the cap based on future needs.

  • Enabled: Authorized identities can increase or decrease the max supply cap.
  • Disabled: Max supply is permanently fixed at deployment.

Governance Safeguards (Max Supply Changes)

Control what modifications are allowed to max supply rules after deployment:

  • Allow changing authorized changer to "No One": Permits permanently locking the max supply by removing all authorized changers.
  • Allow changing rule changer to "No One": Allows rule changers to lock themselves out, making the max supply immutable.
  • Allow rule changer to modify themselves: Enables reassigning control to different identities or groups.

Security tip: Keep disabled for flexibility. Enable only if you plan to make max supply permanently unchangeable later.

History Tracking

Enables on-chain event logging for transfers, mints, burns, freezes, or purchases. More tracking = better transparency, but uses more storage.

Start as Paused

If checked, no one can transfer tokens until you unpause. Useful for pre-launch or staged rollouts.

Allow Transfer to Frozen Balances

Lets users send tokens even to frozen accounts (receiver cannot spend them). Usually used for compliance or escrow-like systems.

Best Practice

  • For public tokens: decimals 8‚Äì18, enable history tracking for at least transfers and mints.
  • For internal or test tokens: 2‚Äì6 decimals, history off for simplicity.
  • Max supply changes: enable if your tokenomics may need future adjustments, disable for immutable hard caps.
  • Governance safeguards: keep disabled unless you need the option to permanently lock max supply later.
  • Always verify your base supply before launch ‚Äî this sets your economy's starting point.

Manual Freeze Permission

Decide if token balances can be frozen (temporarily locked).

Detailed Guide

Enable Freezing

If enabled: Allows blocking a wallet's transfers (balance stays visible but unusable).

If disabled: Ensures full decentralization ‚Äî no one can stop transfers.

Who Can Freeze

  • Owner: Central authority (admin-style)
  • Specific Identity: Targeted compliance officer or regulator
  • Group: Multi-party governance (recommended for fairness)
  • No One: Fully decentralized ‚Äî tokens cannot be frozen

Who Can Change This Rule

If set to "No One," even you cannot restore freeze authority later.

Unfreezing

Choose whether frozen balances can be restored. You can enable unfreezing independently from freezing to create flexible compliance workflows.

Governance Safeguards

Each permission (Freeze, Unfreeze, Destroy Frozen) has independent governance safeguards that control post-deployment modifications:

  • Allow changing authorized actor to "No One": Permits permanently disabling the action by removing all authorized actors.
  • Allow changing rule changer to "No One": Allows rule changers to lock themselves out, making rules immutable.
  • Allow rule changer to modify themselves: Enables rule changers to reassign control to other identities or groups.

Note: Freeze has its own governance safeguards, Unfreeze has separate safeguards, and Destroy Frozen has its own. Configure each independently based on your compliance requirements.

Security tip: Keep safeguards disabled (default) to maintain maximum flexibility. Enable only if you need the option to make permissions permanently immutable later.

Best Practice

  • Regulated tokens (e.g., stablecoins): enable freeze with Group authority.
  • Fully decentralized assets: disable freezing entirely.
  • Compliance workflows: enable both freeze and unfreeze with different authority levels.
  • Governance safeguards: leave disabled for operational flexibility, enable if planning future immutability.
  • If used, always publish freeze policy in your documentation.

Choose a type: Pre-programmed, Perpetual, or None. If active, set cadence ‚Äî by blocks, time, or epoch. This controls the rhythm of emission or rewards. Click Continue to the emission curve.

Distribution Types

  • Pre-programmed: Fixed schedule, set it and forget it
  • Perpetual: Ongoing emission based on rules
  • None: No automatic distribution

Cadence Types

  • Block-Based: Every N blocks (e.g., every 100 blocks)
  • Time-Based: Every N seconds/minutes/hours (e.g., 3600 = 1 hour)
  • Epoch-Based: Per epoch period (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, yearly)

Emission Function

Set the mathematical rule that determines emission over time. Pick a model that matches your economics:

  • Fixed: Constant output
  • Step: Halving or tiered steps
  • Linear: Smooth growth or reduction
  • Exponential / Polynomial / Logarithmic: Advanced curves for adaptive or decreasing emissions

Define your parameters carefully ‚Äî this shapes your token's inflation model.

Emission Types

  1. Fixed Amount

    Same number every time. Simple and predictable. Example: 100 tokens per day.

  2. Exponential Decay

    Decreases over time (like Bitcoin halving). Reduces inflation gradually.

  3. Linear

    Increases or decreases steadily. Example: Start at 100, add 10 each period.

  4. Step Function

    Different amounts for different time periods. Most flexible option.

üí° Pro Tip Fixed is simplest. Exponential creates scarcity over time. Step gives maximum control.

History Tracking

Choose which token events are recorded on-chain.

Guide

Enable tracking for transparency and auditing ‚Äî Transfers, Mints, Burns, Freezes, Purchases. Keep off for privacy or storage optimization. Click Continue for marketplace options.

What Can Be Tracked

  • Transfers: Record when tokens move between addresses
  • Mints: Record when new tokens are created
  • Burns: Record when tokens are destroyed
  • Freezes: Record when addresses are frozen/unfrozen
  • Purchases: Record direct purchase transactions

Trade-offs

More tracking: Better transparency and audit trails, but uses more on-chain storage.

Less tracking: Lower costs and more privacy, but harder to audit or verify activity.

Registration Methods Explained


                
Mobile (QR Code)

                
This is the recommended method for most users. The wizard generates a series of QR codes containing
                  your token configuration. You scan these codes using the Dash mobile wallet app, which handles the
                  identity creation and contract submission automatically.

                
Best for: Users who prefer mobile-first workflows and want a simple, secure process
                  without exposing their recovery phrase to a web browser.


                
DET (Dash Evo Tool)

                
This method provides a step-by-step workflow to download your contract JSON and deploy it using the
                  command-line Dash Evo Tool. You'll get clear instructions for installation and deployment, plus the
                  ability to preview and download both the contract and full wizard configuration.

                
Best for: Developers, advanced users, automated workflows, and those who prefer
                  command-line tools.

                
What you'll do: Download contract JSON ‚Üí Install DET ‚Üí Run deployment command


                
Self-Service (Manual Registration)

                
Import your wallet mnemonic directly into the browser to register your identity and submit the
                  contract in one flow. This is the fastest method but requires you to enter your recovery phrase into a
                  web application.

                
Best for: Technical users who understand the security implications and want
                  immediate registration.

                
Security Note: Only use this method if you understand the risks of entering your
                  recovery phrase in a browser environment.
